# Floristería Calero Opportunity Audit

## Target
- Business: Floristería Calero
- Website: [floristeriacalero.madrid](https://floristeriacalero.madrid/)
- Location: Calle de Cartagena, 119, 28002 Madrid
- Contact: 915 641 846, `caleroflor2004@gmail.com`

## Why this was chosen
This is a good prospect because the business appears real, local, and commercially active, but the website experience is generic enough that a redesign is easy to justify.

Reasons:
- same-day delivery is already part of the offer
- there is a real local address and phone number
- the business has multiple service lines: bouquets, funerary work, hospital deliveries, weddings/events
- the site already has products and clear commercial intent
- the current experience looks like a template storefront instead of a strong local florist brand

## Source pages reviewed
- Homepage: [https://floristeriacalero.madrid/](https://floristeriacalero.madrid/)
- Contact page: [https://floristeriacalero.madrid/contacto/](https://floristeriacalero.madrid/contacto/)

## What the current site already does well
1. It clearly offers same-day delivery in Madrid.
2. It has a real local business identity instead of a pure dropship feel.
3. It mentions more than 20 years of experience.
4. It has good operational trust details on the contact page:
   - reparto propio
   - no shipping in boxes
   - hospital and tanatorio deliveries
5. It already has categories and live commercial intent.

## Main weaknesses observed
1. **The homepage is visually crowded.**
   The current homepage stacks top links, login, search, cart, repeated navigation blocks, category lists, cookie controls, and product grid content with very little hierarchy.

2. **The strongest trust signals are buried.**
   Same-day delivery, local expertise, own delivery service, and 20+ years of experience are present, but they are not framed as the core reasons to choose the business.

3. **The website sells categories, not occasions.**
   For a florist, customers often buy for birthdays, romance, thanks, condolences, weddings, or urgent delivery. The current structure is product-taxonomy heavy instead of occasion-led.

4. **High-value services are under-presented.**
   Weddings/events and funerary work appear as links or categories, but not as strong service pages with their own conversion flow.

5. **The contact page contains excellent FAQ material, but it is not being used as conversion content.**
   Delivery quality, Sundays/festivos, hospitals, and tanatorios are exactly the questions that matter. Those should influence the homepage and service pages, not live only in FAQ text.

6. **The overall design feels generic instead of local and premium.**
   The site behaves like a default store template, which makes the business look less distinctive than it probably is in real life.

## Redesign direction chosen
The redesign concept focuses on:
- clearer local florist positioning
- same-day delivery above the fold
- occasion-based shopping
- dedicated pages for weddings/events and funerary service
- stronger phone/contact CTAs
- more premium visual language
- real business photography instead of invented concept art
- lightweight catalog administration to make the proposal feel operational
- less template clutter

## Key strategic decision
Treat Floristería Calero as a **local trust-first florist with urgent and emotional purchases**, not as a generic catalog store.

That means the site should optimize for:
- fast decision-making
- reassurance
- clear contact paths
- visible local proof

## Sales angle
This is an easy sell because the pitch is not:
"You need a brand new business."

The pitch is:
"You already have the right services and trust assets. The current site just does not present them in the clearest or most premium way."

## What the concept site demonstrates
- a stronger home page
- real-source imagery from the existing business
- purchase by occasion
- cleaner product detail pages
- dedicated service pages for weddings/events and funerary work
- contact and FAQ framed as conversion support
- operational lead forms that prepare structured email requests
- a simple admin panel for product add/edit/delete/import/export
- placement control so the business can decide which flowers get homepage visibility
- a lightweight admin-only gate that separates editing from public browsing

## Best outreach angle
Lead with respect for the existing business, then point out that:
- the current site contains the right commercial ingredients
- the redesign simply packages them better
- the goal is more calls, better local trust, and easier mobile orders
