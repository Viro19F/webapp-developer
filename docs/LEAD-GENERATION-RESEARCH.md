# Lead Generation Website Research

This document captures current research on what makes service-business and landing-page websites convert better.

Research date:
- March 20, 2026

Primary sources used:
- [HubSpot: Form conversion optimization tips](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/optimize-conversion-forms)

## 1. What Strong Lead-Gen Sites Repeatedly Do Well

### A. They make the CTA specific

HubSpot's form research found that generic button language like `Submit` underperforms more specific action language.

What this means:
- button copy is part of conversion design
- every lead form should describe the outcome of clicking

### B. They reduce form friction

HubSpot's landing page and form guidance keeps repeating the same logic:
- fewer fields
- less effort
- clearer value exchange

This matters because service-business visitors often just want to know:
- can this company help me
- how do I reach them quickly
- what happens next

### C. They keep the page aligned around one action

HubSpot's landing-page material emphasizes that even one disengaging element can drop conversions.

Practical takeaway:
- campaign landing pages should not behave like full brochure sites
- one offer, one CTA path, and one clear value story usually work better

### D. They connect the website to the sales conversation

HubSpot's discovery and consultative-selling material reinforces:
- research before outreach
- ask targeted questions
- diagnose the business issue
- connect the solution to the prospect's goals

This matters for websites because the page and the sales process should feel like one system.

### E. They support trust and clarity, not just lead capture

Google's SEO starter guidance and HubSpot's landing-page patterns align on one thing:
- the page needs useful content, clear structure, and relevance to the visitor's intent

Strong lead-gen pages are not just forms on pretty backgrounds.

## 2. Practical Patterns We Should Inherit

### CTA system
- one primary CTA
- action-led wording
- repeat without changing meaning every section

### Form system
- keep fields minimal
- explain the next step
- support autofill
- use visible labels

### Trust system
- reviews
- credentials
- local proof
- team or operator visibility
- response-time expectations when true

### Sales alignment
- the website should answer questions that would otherwise waste the first sales call
- the first sales call should continue the logic of the site, not restart it from zero

---

## 3. What This Means For Our Builds

- We should build more service sites around one dominant action instead of multiple competing calls.
- Landing pages deserve their own structure; they should not just be stripped-down homepages.
- Form labels, button copy, and next-step messaging need much more attention than decorative polish.
- For pitches and audits, the website and outreach process should be designed together.

---

## 4. What Not To Copy Blindly

- do not make every service page into a long generic landing page
- do not over-collect form data
- do not use vague CTA language
- do not separate the website promise from the actual sales process

The lesson is not "use more persuasion tricks".
The lesson is to reduce uncertainty and make the next step feel worthwhile.
