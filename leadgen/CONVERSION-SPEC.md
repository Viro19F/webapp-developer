# Service Website / Lead-Gen Conversion Spec

This document captures the reusable conversion logic for service-business websites and landing pages.

Use it for:
- local service businesses
- B2B service firms
- quote-request sites
- booking-driven sites
- landing pages for campaigns

Related research:
- `docs/LEAD-GENERATION-RESEARCH.md`
- `website/SALES-PLAYBOOK.md`

---

## 1. Core Objective

A lead-generation site should:
1. make the offer clear
2. build trust quickly
3. reduce hesitation
4. make contacting or booking feel easy

The site does not need to tell everything.
It needs to make the next step feel obvious and safe.

---

## 2. Homepage Logic

The first screen should clarify:
- what the business does
- who it helps
- where it operates if location matters
- what action the visitor should take
- why it is trustworthy

Strong homepage structure:
- clear headline
- specific subhead
- primary CTA
- trust strip or proof
- services or outcomes
- proof/testimonials/case evidence
- FAQ or objection handling
- final CTA

---

## 3. CTA Rules

- use one primary CTA
- repeat it consistently
- write the CTA in action language, not generic language
- align CTA wording with the user’s goal

Good examples:
- `Call now`
- `Book inspection`
- `Get a quote`
- `Request proposal`
- `Schedule a visit`

Avoid:
- `Submit`
- `Learn more` as the only action

---

## 4. Form Rules

Lead forms should ask only for what the next step needs.

Default fields:
- name
- phone or email
- one short qualifier
- optional message if needed

Good lead forms:
- stay short
- use visible labels
- use specific button text
- explain what happens after submission
- support autofill
- work well on mobile

---

## 5. Trust Rules

Service sites usually convert on trust before detail.

High-value trust elements:
- real team or operator photos
- reviews and review counts
- service area clarity
- certifications or licenses
- years in business
- guarantees
- before/after proof
- local address and phone
- speed or response-time promise when true

---

## 6. Landing Page Rules

Campaign landing pages should remove distractions.

Use when:
- paid ads
- one specific offer
- one audience segment
- one action

Best practices:
- tighter nav or no full-site nav
- one headline, one offer, one CTA path
- short proof nearby
- form above the fold or close to it
- no generic filler sections

---

## 7. Mobile Lead Rules

For many service businesses, mobile is the primary conversion environment.

That means:
- sticky phone CTA
- large tap targets
- minimal form friction
- fast load
- visible trust immediately
- click-to-call and maps links where relevant

---

## 8. Sales Alignment Rules

The website and the sales process should match.

The site should help answer:
- what problem do we solve
- how do we work
- what should the visitor expect next
- why trust us over alternatives

This makes the later discovery call or quote conversation easier and shorter.

---

## 9. What We Should Reuse Automatically

Future lead-gen builds should default to:
- one clear primary CTA
- short high-intent forms
- trust before detail overload
- mobile-first contact paths
- landing pages with fewer distractions
- explicit next-step language
- sales-ready structure that supports discovery and follow-up

This is the baseline for service sites that generate real leads instead of passive traffic.
