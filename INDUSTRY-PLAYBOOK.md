This is Claude's reference guide. When Fernando says "new client", Claude reads this to build the right site.

## 1. RESTAURANTS & CAFES

### Pages
Homepage, Menu (HTML — NEVER PDF), About, Contact/Location, Online Ordering, Reservations, Gallery, Events, Reviews/Press

### Design
- **Fine dining:** Dark bg + warm accents (gold, terracotta, cream). Serif display fonts (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond)
- **Casual/cafe:** Light bg (cream, white) + green or warm accents. Clean sans-serif
- **Both:** Full-width hero food photography, minimal clutter, generous whitespace

### Colors by type
- Mediterranean/traditional: terracotta, gold, deep red, cream
- Upscale: dark navy/black + gold/amber
- Health/farm-to-table: white + green accents
- Modern/bold: black-and-white + single bright accent

### Must-have features
- HTML menu with categories, descriptions, prices, food photos, dietary icons (V, VG, GF)
- Reservation widget embedded (OpenTable, Resy, or simple form)
- Click-to-call on every page
- Sticky CTA ("Order Now" / "Reserve a Table")
- Google Maps embed
- Hours visible on every page (header or footer)

### Key stats
- 90% of restaurant searches happen on mobile
- 61% of guests say menu photos are most important feature
- PDF menus kill SEO and mobile UX — always use HTML

### SEO
- Google Business Profile optimized
- NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone) across all listings
- Restaurant schema markup (menu items, prices, hours, ratings)
- Local keywords: "[cuisine] restaurant in [city]"

### Common mistakes
- PDF menus, slow loading, stock food photos, outdated hours/menus, auto-playing music, burying contact info

## 2. LAW FIRMS & LEGAL SERVICES

### Pages
Homepage, Practice Areas (1 page PER area), Attorney Profiles, About, Contact, Blog/Legal Insights, Testimonials, FAQ, Case Results

### Design
- **Primary:** Navy blue (trust, authority) + white + gold or charcoal
- **Alternative:** Dark charcoal + white + muted gold
- **Style:** Clean, formal, ample white space
- **Typography:** Serif headings (tradition, trustworthiness) + sans-serif body
- **Imagery:** Real attorney photos (NEVER stock). Professional headshots, office photos

### Must-have features
- "Free Consultation" CTA on every page (above fold + repeated)
- Short intake form: name, email, phone, brief legal issue description
- Click-to-call (60%+ traffic is mobile)
- Practice area pages with case results and specific CTAs
- Live chat or chatbot for after-hours leads
- Attorney bios with credentials, bar admissions, case victories

### Trust signals (critical for law)
- Awards: Super Lawyers, Best Lawyers, Martindale-Hubbell
- Bar association memberships
- Client review counts ("100+ 5-Star Reviews")
- Case results with dollar amounts
- Years of experience
- Media mentions ("As Seen In")

### Legal compliance (REQUIRED)
- "No Attorney-Client Relationship" disclaimer
- "Not Legal Advice" disclaimer
- Jurisdiction disclaimer (which states)
- Advertisement disclaimer (required in many states)
- Privacy Policy
- Case results disclaimer ("Past results do not guarantee future outcomes")

### CTA language
- USE: "Get Your Free Case Review", "Talk to a Lawyer Today", "Start Your Free Consultation"
- AVOID: "Learn More", "Submit", "Contact Us"

### SEO
- 96% of people seeking legal advice use search engines
- Local keywords: "[practice area] lawyer in [city]"
- EEAT signals critical (Google holds legal content to higher standards)
- Google Business Profile + reviews

## 3. DENTAL & MEDICAL CLINICS

### Pages
Homepage, Services (1 page per service), About/Team, Patient Info/New Patients, Contact/Location, Online Booking, Testimonials, Before/After Gallery, Blog, Emergency Info, Insurance & Financing, Privacy Policy

### Design
- **Primary:** Blue (trust, cleanliness) or soft teal
- **Calming alt:** Green (wellness), warm neutrals (beige, warm gray)
- **Background:** White or light gray — clean, spacious
- **Accents:** Soft coral, warm gold for CTAs
- **Typography:** Sans-serif body (Inter, Nunito — readable for older patients), serif headings for authority
- **Imagery:** Real photos of office, team, smiling patients (NOT stock)

### Must-have features
- Online appointment booking (68% of patients prefer online scheduling)
- Click-to-call prominently placed
- Doctor bios with credentials, certifications, personal touch
- Insurance accepted list
- Digital intake forms (HIPAA-compliant)
- Before/after gallery (especially cosmetic/orthodontic)
- Google Reviews integration

### Trust signals
- ADA membership, board certifications
- Years in practice / patients served
- Before/after photos
- Google/Yelp star ratings
- "New Patient Special" offers

### Compliance
- HIPAA: SSL/TLS encryption, BAAs with all vendors touching patient data, privacy policy, minimum necessary data collection
- ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility: 4.5:1 contrast ratio, alt text, keyboard navigation, resizable text (DEADLINE: May 11, 2026)
- No standard analytics on pages collecting health info without proper BAAs

### Key stats
- 60%+ traffic is mobile
- Top dental sites convert 10% of visitors to patients (average 3-5%)
- Keep initial contact forms to 4 fields max

## 4. BEAUTY SALONS & BARBERSHOPS

### Pages
Homepage, Services + Pricing, Team/Stylists (with direct booking links), Gallery/Portfolio, Testimonials, Contact/Location, Blog, Products/Shop (optional)

### Design — Salon/Spa
- **Luxury salon:** Neutrals (beige, cream, white) + gold or rose gold accents
- **Feminine beauty:** Soft pinks, blush, dusty rose + gold
- **Spa/wellness:** Sage green, soft blue, muted teal, earth tones
- **Style:** Elegant, generous whitespace, serene feel
- **Typography:** Oversized serif display fonts for headings + clean sans-serif body
- **Imagery:** High-quality real photos, consistent lighting, video hero sections

### Design — Barbershop
- **Colors:** Deep charcoal, navy, dark wood tones + amber/copper accents
- **Style:** Masculine, vintage-inspired or modern-industrial
- **Typography:** Bold, clean sans-serif or vintage display fonts

### Must-have features
- Online booking system (Fresha, Booksy, Vagaro, Square Appointments)
- Booking CTA above fold, in nav, and at end of every section
- Before/after gallery organized by service type
- Instagram feed embed (96% of beauty brands are on Instagram)
- Transparent pricing (hidden prices erode trust)
- Individual stylist profiles with specialties and direct booking

### Gallery best practices
- Before/after with matching lighting, angle, background
- Clean neutral background
- Organize by service type (color, cuts, treatments)
- No heavy filters — brightness/contrast only
- Pair with client testimonials
- Get written client permission

## 5. GYMS & FITNESS

### Pages
Homepage, Classes/Schedule, Membership/Pricing, Trainers/Coaches, About, Facilities/Virtual Tour, Success Stories, Free Trial page, Contact/Location, Blog

### Design
- **High-energy gym:** Red + black, or orange + dark gray, or electric blue + black
- **Premium fitness:** Near-black + gold accents
- **Boutique/yoga/pilates:** Warm neutrals, sage green, dusty pink (closer to spa)
- **Style:** Bold, energetic, dark backgrounds, high contrast
- **Typography:** Bold sans-serif (Montserrat, Oswald, Bebas Neue) often ALL-CAPS headings
- **Imagery:** Dynamic action shots, athletes mid-workout, real member photos

### Must-have features
- Interactive class schedule (embed from Mindbody, Glofox, Zen Planner)
- Online membership signup (increases conversion 35%)
- Free trial landing page with minimal friction (name, email, phone)
- Membership pricing comparison (3 tiers: Basic, Mid, Premium)
- Highlight most popular tier with badge + visual emphasis
- Trainer profiles with certifications and booking links

### Pricing display
- 3 tiers side-by-side with checkmarks
- Show actual prices (no "call for pricing")
- Monthly vs annual comparison
- Time-limited offers for urgency

### Class schedule display
- Filterable by class type, instructor, day, time
- Color-coded by class type
- Show instructor name/photo
- Show spots remaining (urgency)
- One-click booking from schedule

## 6. REAL ESTATE AGENCIES

### Pages
Homepage, Property Listings, Property Detail pages, Neighborhood/Community pages, About/Team, Blog/Market Insights, Contact, Home Valuation page, Buyer/Seller Resources, Testimonials

### Design
- **Primary:** Blue (trust) — navy for luxury, brighter for modern
- **Accent:** Red or orange for CTAs (urgency)
- **Style:** Clean, minimalist, large photography, generous white space
- **Typography:** Serif for luxury/editorial, sans-serif for modern/approachable
- **Rule:** 60-30-10 color ratio

### Must-have features
- Property search with filters (price, location, beds, type)
- Interactive map search
- Virtual tours / 3D walkthroughs (Matterport)
- Lead capture on every page
- Home valuation form (primary seller lead magnet)
- Mortgage/affordability calculator
- Agent profiles with sales volume and certifications

### Trust signals
- Sales volume and market rankings
- Agent certifications (CRS, ABR, GRI)
- Transaction count / years in business
- Client testimonials with photos

## 7. CONSTRUCTION COMPANIES

### Pages
Homepage, Services (1 per type), Project Portfolio/Gallery, Case Studies, About/Team, Testimonials, Blog, Careers, Contact, Client Portal (optional)

### Design
- **Primary:** Dark blue or navy (trust, stability)
- **Accents:** Orange, yellow, or red (energy, action, construction equipment reference)
- **Luxury construction:** Dark charcoal + gold
- **Eco/sustainable:** Green + earth tones
- **Style:** Bold, industrial, large project photography, dark overlays with white text
- **Typography:** Bold sans-serif headings, grid layouts for portfolio

### Must-have features
- Project portfolio (THE most important page — 47% more inquiries with portfolios)
- Before/after photo sliders
- Case studies: photos, scope, timeline, budget range, challenges solved
- Quote request form (project type, scope, budget range, timeline)
- Service area map
- Safety and certification badges

### Trust signals
- Licenses (state contractor license, OSHA, LEED)
- Insurance/bonding info
- Years in business, projects completed
- Safety record / incident-free hours
- Industry memberships (AGC, NAHB, ABC)
- BBB accreditation

## 8. HOME SERVICES (Plumbing, Electrician, HVAC)

### Pages
Homepage, Individual Service pages (1 per service for SEO), Emergency Services page, About/Team, Service Area, Reviews, Before/After Gallery, Pricing/Estimates, Blog, Contact, Financing page

### Design
- **Plumbing:** Blue (water) + white + green or orange accents
- **Electrician:** Yellow/amber (electricity) + dark navy + white
- **HVAC:** Blue (cooling) + red/orange (heating) + white
- **Style:** Clean, bright, trustworthy. NOT dark — homeowners want to feel safe
- **Typography:** Clear sans-serif. Clarity > style
- **Imagery:** Uniformed technicians, branded trucks, smiling pros

### Must-have features
- Click-to-call sticky button (THE primary conversion on mobile)
- "24/7 Emergency" messaging prominently displayed
- Emergency floating button (red/orange)
- Online booking/scheduling with date/time selection
- Service area map with covered neighborhoods
- Before/after galleries by service type
- Response time guarantee ("We arrive within 60 minutes")

### Trust signals
- Licenses and registration numbers (often legally required)
- Insurance and bonding
- Background-checked/drug-tested technicians
- Manufacturer certifications (Carrier, Lennox for HVAC)
- Google/Yelp star ratings with review count
- BBB accreditation
- "Satisfaction guaranteed" badge

### Key stats
- 64%+ traffic is mobile
- 5-min response time vs 30-min = massive revenue difference
- Lead cost: $25-$300 depending on service type

## 9. E-COMMERCE / RETAIL (Small Shops)

### Pages
Homepage, Product Catalog, Product Detail pages, Cart, Checkout (3 steps max), About, Contact, Shipping & Delivery, Returns & Refunds, FAQ, Privacy/Terms
- If the shop has a physical location: add Visit / Tasting / Store Hours page

### Design
- Varies by brand — match the product aesthetic
- Clean white backgrounds for product photos
- Grid layout: 3-4 columns desktop, 2 mobile
- Large product images, minimal clutter
- Trust badges near checkout buttons
- For curated specialty retail, explain the shelf logic clearly ("for pure bowls", "for lattes", "for gifts")
- If photography is not ready yet, use branded pack illustrations or clean vector mockups instead of generic stock images

### Must-have features
- Product grid with filters (size, color, price, category) + sorting
- Quick view modals
- High-quality product photography (multiple angles, lifestyle shots)
- Reviews on product pages (88% trust online reviews)
- Simple checkout: guest checkout, 3 steps max, progress indicator
- Multiple payment options (card, PayPal, Apple Pay)
- For first-time-buyer categories (tea, coffee, skincare, specialty food), add product guidance: tasting notes, best-use case, and starter recommendations
- For physical retail, include a soft-conversion CTA like tasting reservation, consultation, or in-store pickup

### Payment integration (no Shopify needed)
- **Snipcart:** 2 lines of code, full cart/checkout, works with any HTML site, 2%/transaction
- **Stripe Checkout:** Redirect to Stripe-hosted page, cheapest fees (~2.9%+30c)
- **PayPal Buttons:** Easy embed, trusted, good as secondary option
- Best combo for small store: Snipcart + PayPal, or Stripe Checkout + PayPal

### Product photography (DIY)
- Natural light near large window
- White background for product shots
- Lifestyle shots showing product in use
- Tripod for consistency
- Multiple angles + detail close-ups
- Compress for web (<200KB per image, WebP format)

### Cart abandonment killers
- Hidden costs (#1 reason — 50% of abandonments)
- Forced account creation
- Too many checkout steps
- No guest checkout
- Slow loading

### SEO
- Product schema markup (JSON-LD): name, image, price, availability, rating
- Unique product descriptions (never copy manufacturer text)
- Clean URLs: /products/leather-wallet not /products?id=47
- Alt text on all product images
- If there is a physical store, add local intent copy: neighborhood, city, store hours, pickup/tasting details

### Specialty retail notes (matcha, tea, coffee, niche food)
- Customers often buy by use case, not by brand: ceremonial, daily, latte, tools, gifts
- Education must live inside the product experience: tasting notes, caffeine feel, how to use it, who it is for
- A small, opinionated catalog often converts better than a large, noisy one
- Starter bundles are high-conversion because they remove the "what else do I need?" question
- If the store is physical-first, "Book tasting" or "Visit the counter" can outperform a hard-sell CTA on the first page

## UNIVERSAL RULES (ALL INDUSTRIES)

### Mobile
- Mobile-first design is mandatory for every industry (60-70%+ of traffic)
- Load time under 3 seconds
- Tap targets minimum 44x44px
- Click-to-call phone numbers
- Sticky CTA button on mobile
- Hamburger menu on mobile

### SEO basics (every site)
- Google Business Profile (for local businesses)
- NAP consistency across all listings
- Schema markup appropriate to industry
- Meta titles and descriptions on every page
- Alt text on all images
- Clean URL structure
- Fast page speed (Core Web Vitals)
- SSL certificate (HTTPS)

### Trust (every site)
- Real photos, not stock
- Customer reviews/testimonials
- Clear contact info visible on every page
- Professional, consistent design
- Privacy policy
- Social media links

### Conversion (every site)
- Clear CTA on every page
- CTA above the fold
- Multiple contact methods (phone, form, chat, WhatsApp)
- Simple forms (minimize required fields)
- Social proof near CTAs
- Fast response commitment ("We'll call you within 24 hours")

### What NOT to do (any site)
- Auto-playing audio/video with sound
- Stock photos of generic people
- "Submit" as a button label
- Hidden contact info
- Slow loading (>3 seconds)
- Outdated content
- Spelling/grammar errors
- Cluttered layout
- Too many fonts (max 2)
- Missing mobile optimization
