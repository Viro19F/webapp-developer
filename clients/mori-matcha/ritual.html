Store philosophy

Matcha should feel clear, not intimidating.

Mori Matcha exists for the customer who wants to drink better tea without memorizing a textbook. We guide people through grade, texture, and tools in plain language.

What we believe

Fewer products. Better explanation.

Most first-time buyers do not need ten ceremonial grades. They need a calm point of view, a good whisk, and one bowl that tastes right on day one.

Sourcing

Stone-milled where it matters. Edited where it matters more.

The shelf focuses on trusted Japanese producers for ceremonial and everyday matcha, then narrows the line further based on drinkability, texture, and repeat use.

If a tin only tastes good in a perfect ceramic bowl on a perfect morning, it does not earn the shelf.

How we sort the shelf

By use case, not by jargon.

Ceremonial is for pure bowls. Everyday is for repeat cups. Latte blends are for milk. Tools solve texture. Treats soften the entry point.

That structure helps buyers understand what to buy fast and keeps reorder decisions simple.

Whisking guide

Three steps to a better first bowl.

Simple enough to repeat every morning and tight enough to teach in-store in under two minutes.

1

Measure lightly

Use 2 grams for a straight bowl or 3 to 4 grams if you are building a latte.

2

Water before heat

Keep water below a boil. Too hot and the cup turns sharp before the sweetness has a chance to show.

3

Finish with food

Something tiny and citrus-led helps new drinkers read the cup as clean and complex, not bitter.
